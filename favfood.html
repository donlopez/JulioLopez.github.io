Cazuela de Frijoles

My favorite dish, "Cazuela de Frijoles," holds a special place in my heart, embodying the rich, cultural tapestry of Colombia. This dish is a testament to the traditional Colombian cuisine, offering a harmonious blend of flavors that speaks volumes of its culinary heritage. The combination of red beans, pork, avocado, and arepas, among other ingredients, creates a comforting and hearty meal that is both nourishing and satisfying. Its deliciousness lies not just in the taste but in the memories and feelings it evokes, making it an unparalleled favorite.

Cazuelita de Frijoles (Colombian Beans Cazuela)

Preparation time: 15 minutes
Cooking time: 30 minutes
Total: 45 minutes
Diners: 6 servings

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups red beans
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 910 grams (2 pounds) ground beef
  • 1 cup of hogao or stew
  • 910 grams (2 pounds) pork rinds
  • 6 eggs
  • 6 sausages
  • 3 avocados
  • arepas
  • 4 large onions
  • 4 tomatoes finely chopped
  • 3 cups of pre-cooked corn flour
  • 450 grams (1 pound) rice cooked
  • 2 cups of warm water
  • 3 ripe bananas peeled and cut
  • Salt for seasoning to taste

Preparation:

  1. We soak the beans in water one night before preparing them so they will be softer and cook faster.
  2. In a pressure cooker, cook the beans in the water in which they were soaked and add 2 tablespoons of oil.
  3. In a frying pan, saut√© onion and tomato with a little salt to prepare the hogao.
  4. Cook our hogao for 10 minutes then fry the ground meat in oil over medium heat, adding half of the hogao and stir well. Reserve the other half of the hogao.
  5. When the beans are soft, add salt to taste the remaining stew and let it cook for 5 to 10 minutes to thicken.
  6. Fry the pork rinds in another pan until they are toasted or however we like.
  7. Finally, put oil in a frying pan to fry the ripe bananas and eggs.
  8. To prepare the arepas, in a large bowl mix the corn flour, a tablespoon of oil, salt to taste, and pour water little by little over the mixture until you get a consistent and homogeneous dough. Make balls with the dough and flatten it with our hands until it forms an arepa.
  9. Put a little oil in a frying pan and grill the arepas until they are golden brown on both sides. Enjoy your cazuela paisa.
